About

The Red Fort, or Lal Qila, is a historic fortification in Old Delhi, India. Commissioned by Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in 1638, it served as the main residence of the Mughal emperors for nearly 200 years.

Address

Netaji Subhash Marg, Chandni Chowk, New Delhi, Delhi 110006, India.

Architect

The Red Fort was designed by Ustad Ahmad Lahori, the same architect who is credited with designing the Taj Mahal.

Hours

Open: Tuesday to Sunday Closed: Mondays Timings: Sunrise to Sunset

Visitors

Between April and July 2024, the Red Fort attracted 758,118 visitors, a slight decrease from 770,846 during the same period in 2023. Factors such as heatwaves and unexpected rains contributed to this decline.
