Taj Mahal

  • The Taj Mahal is a stunning white marble mausoleum located in Agra, India.
  • It was built by the Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his beloved wife Mumtaz Mahal, who died in 1631.
  • Construction began in 1632 and was completed in 1648, though some parts of the complex were finished later.
  • The Taj Mahal is a masterpiece of Mughal architecture, blending Persian, Indian, and Islamic styles.
  • It stands on the southern bank of the Yamuna River and is part of a larger complex that includes a mosque, a guesthouse, and beautifully landscaped gardens.
  • One of the most striking features of the Taj Mahal is its massive central dome, which rises 73 meters (240 feet) and is surrounded by four elegant minarets.
  • The entire structure is adorned with intricate carvings, calligraphy, and precious stone inlays.


India gate

  • The India Gate is a war memorial located in New Delhi, India.
  • It was built to honor the 90,000 Indian soldiers who lost their lives fighting for the British Indian Army during World War I and the Third Anglo-Afghan War.
  • Designed by Sir Edwin Lutyens, the monument was completed in 1931 and stands as a symbol of bravery and sacrifice.
  • Key Features of India Gate:
  • Architectural Style: Inspired by the Arc de Triomphe in Paris, it is made of sandstone and stands 42 meters (138 feet) tall.
  • Engraved Names: The names of over 13,000 soldiers are inscribed on its walls.
  • Amar Jawan Jyoti: A perpetual flame (added in 1972) to honor soldiers who died in the Indo-Pak War of 1971.
  • Surrounding Area: It is located on the Rajpath, surrounded by lush cliffs, making it a popular spot for visitors.


Jama masjid

  • The Jama Masjid is a stunning mosque located in Old Delhi, India.
  • It is one of the largest mosques in India and is considered a symbol of Islamic worship and cultural heritage.
  • The mosque was built by the Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan between 1644 and 1656 and designed by architect Ustad Khalil.
  • Key Features of Jama Masjid:
  • Massive Courtyard: The mosque can accommodate over 25,000 worshippers at a time.
  • Three Grand Domes: Made of white marble with intricate black stripes, the domes rest atop the prayer hall.
  • Two Tall Minarets: Standing at 40 meters (131 feet), these minarets are made of alternating red sandstone and white marble and offer a breathtaking view of Old Delhi.
  • Main Entrance Gates: The mosque has three grand entrance gates facing east, north, and south.
  • Intricate Calligraphy and Inlay Work: The walls are adorned with verses from the Quran, beautifully inscribed in Persian calligraphy.


Red fort

  • The Red Fort is a historic fort located in Delhi, India.
  • It was built by the Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in 1639 and served as the capital of the Mughal Empire.
  • Key Features of Red Fort:
  • Architectural Style: The fort is a symmetrical structure with a square base and a tall circular tower.
  • Construction Materials: It is made of red sandstone and marble, with intricate carvings and inscriptions.
  • Historical Significance: The fort is considered one of the most important landmarks in the city of Delhi and is a UNESCO World Heritage Site.


Golden temple

  • The Golden Temple is a Hindu temple located in Amritsar, Punjab, India.
  • It is the spiritual home of the Sikhism religion and is dedicated to the Lord Gurdwara Sahib, the spiritual leader of Sikhism.
  • Key Features of Golden Temple:
  • Architectural Style: The temple is a symmetrical structure with a square base and a tall circular tower.
  • Construction Materials: It is made of red sandstone and marble, with intricate carvings and inscriptions.
  • Historical Significance: The temple is considered one of the most important religious sites in India and is a UNESCO World Heritage Site.
  • Religious Significance: The temple is dedicated to the Lord Gurdwara Sahib, the spiritual leader of Sikhism.
  • Sacred Sarovar: A large holy water tank surrounds the temple, where devotees take a dip for spiritual cleansing.
  • Four Entrances: Symbolizing openness and equality, welcoming people of all religions.
  • Guru Granth Sahib: The holy scripture of Sikhism is placed inside and recited daily.
  • Langar (Community Kitchen): The world's largest free kitchen, serving food to over 100,000 people daily, regardless of caste, creed, or religion.
